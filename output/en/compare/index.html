Decision criteria

What the comparison system measures

Each criterion turns a vague travel preference into a publishable decision signal.

01 24%

Constraint Fit

Measures whether the format remains viable when real-world limits tighten.

Low: Breaks down quickly under pressure. High: Remains workable under real limits.
02 16%

Operational Complexity

Measures the amount of operational friction built into the travel format.

Low: Operationally heavy and coordination-intensive. High: Simple, legible, and easier to execute.
03 14%

Control vs Support

Measures whether the format either over-constrains the traveler or abandons them to excessive self-management.

Low: Either too rigid or too unsupported. High: Balanced autonomy with practical support.
04 14%

Depth of Experience

Measures whether the format produces only managed exposure or a fuller lived experience.

Low: Mostly surface-level or tightly managed. High: Enables richer, more lived travel experience.
05 16%

Predictability

Measures how stable and disruption-resistant the format is in practice.

Low: High variance and easier to derail. High: Stable and more likely to deliver as intended.
06 16%

Traveler Type Fit

Measures whether the format serves a clearly defined traveler profile rather than pretending to suit everyone equally.

Low: Weak or ambiguous fit by traveler profile. High: Strong fit for clearly defined traveler types.

How comparison works

A structured path from preference to fit

The compare layer starts with style behavior, applies weighted criteria, then explains the tradeoff instead of hiding it behind a single recommendation.

01 Define the travel structures

Each style is treated as an operating model with its own cost, control, support, and depth profile.

02 Apply the criteria

The same criteria are used across styles so the comparison stays consistent and repeatable.

03 Explain the tradeoff

The output describes why one style may fit better under a specific traveler constraint.
